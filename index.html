Logan Theodore

User Experience Research


I seek to create things that make life better, things that make a difference. I started by studying industrial design, then shifted into applied research--learning methods to understand and evaluate intractable problems like poverty and education inequity. Interviewing and stakeholder mapping in East Palo Alto led to recommendations for investments. Ethnography with village businesses in rural Mexico led to deep insights and plan of action for supporting organizations.

A bias for action led me to consulting where I researched, concepted and built solutions to improve lives for people in poverty. That same bias for action led me to the classroom where I built a pipeline into magnet schools and future opportunity for a community with growing prospects.

In my user experience research I combine research methods and a bias for action to deliver insights that help make things make a difference.
